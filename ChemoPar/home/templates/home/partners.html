Chemokines display interactions with a rang of different partners, the most important of which we have highlighted in Figure 1:

  • G Protein-Coupled Receptors (GPCRs) are the best known binding partner of chemokines. By inserting the chemokine N-terminus in the orthosteric binding pocket, downstream signaling is activated.
  • Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) present a series of negatively charged polysaccharides. GAGs are mostly present at the cellular surface. There, they bind chemokines and localize them.
  • Peptides are also known to bind chemokines. Some are even used in clinial studies.
  • Small molecules have also been shown to bind chemokines and sometimes even reduce chemokine-mediated signaling.
