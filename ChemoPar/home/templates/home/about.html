About ChemoPar-db

ChemoPar-db: a database of chemokines and their binding partners.

Data overview of ChemoPar-db

ChemoPar-db consists of three main datasets:
  • Sequence data on all chemokines from the PFam family (PF00048)
  • Structural data of chemokines obtained from the PDB
  • Chemokine-Partner interaction fingerprints

Detailed data collection and processing method

ChemoPardb contains all available chemokine PDB structures which are organized, annotated, and aligned. You can Browse the UniProt reviewed chemokine protein entries or you can Search the database for all structures.

Chemokines are a family of small signaling proteins (chemotactic cytokines) that indeed play crucial roles in cellular signaling processes, particularly in the immune system. They are involved in guiding and regulating the movement of immune cells within the body, helping to orchestrate the immune response to various pathogens and inflammatory signals.

ChemoPardb contains all available chemokine PDB structures which are organized, annotated, and aligned. You can Browse the UniProt reviewed chemokine protein entries or you can Search the database for all structures.

Data collection

Chemokine protein entries were collected using the PFAM interleukin-8 family (PF00048) where we focused only on the reviewed UniProt IDs. This resulted in a total of 195 chemokine proteins from different species. These proteins were used to obtain the 227 PDB structures. The selected PDB structures are first split into all available models (specifically for NMR structures).


Structure processing

The structures are protonated with the use of ProteinPlus ProToss webserver1 using an API request.Subsequently, the structures are split into protein, solvent, cofactors, ions, small molecules, and others. The protein elements are further divided into chemokines and partners.
